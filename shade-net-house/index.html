What is Shade Net house?

Shade Net House is a structure enclosed by agricultural mesh or any other woven material to pass through the required sunlight, humidity, and air gaps. This creates a favorable environment for plant growth.

Use of Shade Net house

  • shade net house helps in the cultivation of flower plants, leaf plants, herbs, vegetables, and spices.
  • Fruits and vegetables are used for nurseries as well as for growing forest plant species.
  • Used to protect against insect attacks. Helps to get various agricultural quality products.
  • Helps to increase agricultural yields Protects against natural weather disasters like wind, rain, hail, and frost.
  • It is used for seedling production and to reduce its mortality during summer days.

Agricultural products grown in Shade Net house

mushrooms, bamboo, green leafy vegetables, microgreens, cucumber, pepper, Swiss chard, squash, lemon, orange, grape, strawberry, raspberry, chili, okra, sweet potato, melon, cabbage, Sprouts, sweet corn, broccoli, leeks, pumpkins, carrots, garlic, herbs and many other kinds of flowers.

How does shade-net house help in crop growth?

The Shade Net house controls temperature and air movement as well as the amount of carbon dioxide in the air so that the crop grows well with the right environment for crop growth. The shade net house also helps in maintaining soil moisture and also helps in saving water. Due to the use of polyhouse farmers per hectare

Points to consider before erecting a shade-net house

Site selection –

The shade net house should be located in a place that is well connected to the market for the supply of manufactured goods and sale of products. shade net houses should be built away from buildings and trees, as well as away from industrial or vehicle pollutants.

Direction –

There are two criteria for choosing a polyhouse direction: wind direction and light uniformity. The structure of the polyhouse can be east-west or north-south facing. But to ensure uniform light intensity, it should be towards the north-south direction.

Materials required for making shade-net poly-house

A polyhouse made from an MS frame can last up to 20 to 25 years. If rust treatment is done at regular intervals, a polyhouse made using bamboo can last up to 3 years. The shed net lasts 3 to 5 years depending on the weather conditions. Shed nets are available in a variety of colors with shed percentages ranging from 25%, 30%, 35%, 50%, 60%, 75%, and 90% and should be selected according to the temperature of the space. For example, 75-90% shade net should be used in very hot areas and 50% to 75% shade should be used in submerged areas.
